# Call for Participation: XRWay’26

**The 1st International Workshop on XR on the Way (XRWay’26)**  
**XR for Walking, Driving, and Everyday Mobility**  
Held in conjunction with **IEEE ISMAR 2026**, Bari, Italy, **October 5, 2026**.  
Format: **Hybrid**.

## Overview

Extended Reality (XR) is increasingly used by mobile users such as pedestrians, drivers, passengers, and transit riders, yet most XR systems remain designed and evaluated under the assumption of a seated user in a controlled indoor space. As smart glasses, head-worn displays, and in-vehicle visualization systems become widely available, XR must increasingly support, and remain robust to, real-world locomotion.

XRWay’26 is a focused venue for new work on XR designed for, or robust to, such conditions. We invite original research papers, work-in-progress reports, position papers, case studies, and demos on XR systems, content, interaction techniques, and human factors related to walking, driving, and everyday in-the-wild use of XR.

## Topics of interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

- XR for pedestrians and micromobility
- In-vehicle and in-transit XR
- Large-scale and generative XR content for outdoor environments
- Perceptual coherence in motion, including sensory conflict, cybersickness, and synchronization / de-synchronization
- Safety, attention, accessibility, and context-adaptive interfaces
- Wearable haptics and robotic actuators for ambulatory and in-vehicle feedback
- Long-term, everyday XR in-the-wild, including privacy and social acceptability
- Empirical studies, field deployments, datasets, and benchmarks for mobile XR

## Submission tracks

### Regular Research Papers

Up to **8 pages excluding references**. Suitable for mature research contributions, evaluated systems, empirical studies, technical methods, datasets, benchmarks, or design frameworks.

### Position & Work-in-Progress Papers

Up to **4 pages excluding references**. Suitable for provocative positions, early-stage projects, negative results, open challenges, methods papers, or work-in-progress reports.

### Case Study & Demo Papers

Up to **4 pages excluding references**. Suitable for field deployments, design cases, prototypes, toolkits, or lightweight AR/MR demos suitable for the workshop’s walk-and-talk session.

## Formatting and review

Submissions should use the IEEE Computer Society VGTC conference format: <https://tc.computer.org/vgtc/publications/conference/>.

All submissions will undergo single-blind peer review. Accepted papers are planned for publication in the ISMAR 2026 Adjunct Proceedings and IEEE Xplore. At least one author of each accepted paper must register and present on-site at ISMAR 2026 in Bari, Italy.

Submissions will be handled via EasyChair. The submission link will be added upon workshop acceptance.
